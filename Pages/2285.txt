RockfordRockford
The University of Illinois College of Medicine at Rockford provides  world-class medical training and offers health related services to  Northern Illinois. The College of Medicine is internationally recognized  as a leader in medical education and research. The College of Medicine  at Rockford serves as the principal educator of health service  professionals as a major healthcare provider for Rockford and the  underserved communities in northern Illinois.  Among the programs  offered at the Rockford campus include the Master of Science in  Biotechnology (MBT) and Rural Medical Education (RMED) Program.  The  campus consists of 20 acres in the heart of Rockford, Illinois.
About the Rockford Community
The city of Rockford is home to almost 300,000 people and is the  third largest city in the state of Illinois. The Rock River flows  through the middle of town and provides the city with its name. Rockford  is also known as the “Forest City” for its abundance of trees and  assorted greenery.  In addition to UIC’s College of Medicine, the city’s  major employers include other healthcare providers, including Swedish  American and Saint Francis Hospitals.
